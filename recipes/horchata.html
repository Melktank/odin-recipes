Horchata

Horchata is a traditional Mexican drink made up of white rice soaked in water, it's flavored with cinnamon and its sweetened with granulated sugar. Everything is minced up together in a blender and later strained to remove solids.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup white rice uncooked
  • 5 cups of Water
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 1 pinch of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1 can of condensed milk
  • 1/2 tablespoon of vanilla extract

Steps

  1. In a medium size pot, combine the rice, water, condensed milk, almond milk, cinnamon sticks, cinnamon, and salt, bring to a boil.
  2. Reduce the heat to a low simmer, cover, and allow to cook for 25 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat and allow the mixture to cool to room temperature.
  4. Remove the cinnamon sticks and add the rice mixture to a blender along with the remaining ingredients.
  5. Blend on high for 1 to 3 minutes until very smooth.
  6. Chill it or served over ice.
